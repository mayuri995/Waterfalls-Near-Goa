All about Nagartas Waterfall

Nangartas Waterfall is a natural waterfall located in the Amboli region of Maharashtra, India, known for its narrow gorge and the force with which the water plunges down from a height of about 40 feet. It is a popular tourist attraction, especially during the monsoon season when the waterfall is at its most powerful. The area surrounding the waterfall is known for its lush greenery, making it a scenic spot for nature lovers.

Key Features:

Narrow Gorge:

The waterfall cascades through a narrow gorge, creating a unique and dramatic effect.

Monsoon Spectacle:

During the monsoon season, the waterfall is at its most powerful, with a roaring sound and a spray of water.

Scenic Location:

The waterfall is surrounded by lush greenery and offers a peaceful and refreshing experience.

It's easily accessible, being located just off the state highway, about 10 km from Amboli.

There are two viewing areas built by the local authority from where visitors can safely observe the waterfall.

Farmer Statue:

A statue of a farmer is also a notable feature near the waterfall.

    Visiting Tips:
  • Best Time to Visit:

    • The monsoon season (July to September) is the most popular time to witness the waterfall in full force, but the area is accessible from July to March.

  • Safety Precautions:

    • Be cautious near the edge of the gorge, as it can be steep and dangerous.

  • Photography:

    • The waterfall is a great spot for photography, but be mindful of the spray and the steepness of the terrain.

  • Other Attractions:

    • While in the area, consider exploring other attractions in Amboli, such as the Amboli Sunset Point and Kavleshet Point,
