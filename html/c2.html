All about chikhale Waterfall


Chikhale Waterfall is a picturesque natural attraction located near Chikhale village, approximately 40 kilometers from Belagavi city in Karnataka, India. Nestled within a thick forest, it offers a serene escape into nature, especially captivating during the monsoon season when the waterfall reaches its peak flow and beauty.

Accessibility and trekking

Reaching the waterfall involves a 2.5-kilometer trek through lush forests after parking your vehicle about 500 meters from Chikhale Village, which is 3 km from the Jamboti - Peeranwadi road junction. The moderately easy trek features misty trails and picturesque landscapes. An alternative, more challenging route through Parwad is also available for adventurous trekkers.

Best time to visit

The monsoon season (July to September) is the ideal time to experience Chikhale Waterfall's full splendor.

Important tips

  • Start your journey early and plan to leave by 4 PM.
  • Carry sufficient water and snacks.
  • Wear comfortable trekking shoes.
  • Keep emergency contacts handy.
  • Do not litter the area; carry back all garbage and plastics to the village.
  • Exercise caution near the waterfall as the rocks can be slippery, and the water flow can become strong during heavy rain.

Safety concerns

  • Despite the waterfall's beauty, safety concerns exist due to tourists ignoring safety rules and venturing into dangerous areas near the falls. Weak fencing implemented by the Forest Department is easily crossed, leading to risky behavior like taking selfies and reels at the edge. Although an entry fee has been implemented to manage the area and improve safety, its enforcement is reportedly weak due to a shortage of trained staff and inadequate fencing. Visitors are urged to prioritize safety and adhere to any restrictions or guidelines in place.
  • In June 2025, the Karnataka Forest Department reportedly imposed a "No Entry" ban on all waterfalls in Khanapur taluka, including Chikhale, due to safety concerns during the rainy season. However, reports also indicate that the falls were reopened with paid entry due to persistent tourist inflow through unauthorized routes and staffing shortages. Visitors should verify the current status of access restrictions before planning a visit.
