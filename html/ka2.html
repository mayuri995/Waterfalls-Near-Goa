All about Kawalesad Waterfall

Kavlesad Waterfall, also known as Kavaleshet Point, is a scenic viewpoint and waterfall located in Amboli, Maharashtra. It's famous for its lush greenery, particularly during the monsoon season, and the unique phenomenon of reverse waterfalls caused by strong winds. The area offers panoramic views of the Western Ghats, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and photographers.

Key features of Kavlesad Waterfall:

Reverse Waterfalls:

During heavy rainfall and strong winds, the water flowing down the mountain can appear to flow upwards due to the wind's force. This creates a unique and mesmerizing spectacle.

Panoramic Views:

The viewpoint offers stunning vistas of the surrounding valleys and mountains, especially during the monsoon season when the area is covered in mist.

Monsoon Beauty:

Amboli, and particularly Kavlesad, is known for its beauty during the monsoon season (June to September), with lush greenery and frequent cloud cover.

Photography Hotspot:

The area is popular among photographers and videographers due to its scenic landscapes and the reverse waterfall phenomenon.

Accessibility:

Kavlesad Point is easily accessible by road from Amboli and nearby towns. You can find autos from the bus stand to reach the viewpoint. <>

Nearby Attractions:

The area also features the Hiranyakeshi Temple, a cave, and other viewpoints offering stunning scenery.

Things to keep in mind:

Crowds:

During weekends and holidays, the area can get crowded, and traffic jams are common, especially near the waterfall.

Safety:

Be cautious when near the waterfall, especially during heavy rainfall, as there can be slippery rocks and strong currents.

Monsoon Precautions:<>

Landslides are a possibility during the monsoon season, so it's advisable to stay updated on weather conditions and road closures.

Cleanliness:

While the area is beautiful, some visitors have noted concerns about littering.
