All about Shimbola Waterfall


Shimbola Falls, nestled in the Belgaum district of Karnataka, India, is a picturesque waterfall located in the heart of the Western Ghats near Chorla. It's a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts alike.

Here's an overview of Shimbola Falls:

Location

Belgaum district, Karnataka, India. Part of the Western Ghats. Near Chorla.

Approximately 50 km from Belagavi, on the Goa-Karnataka border.

Features

  • Stunning 200-foot waterfall.
  • Fed by the Shimboli stream, creating a roaring sound.
  • Surrounded by dense forests, deep valleys, and mist-covered treetops, especially during the monsoon season.
  • Known for its lush rainforest environment with diverse ivy.
  • Clear and clean water.
  • Area around the falls is home to a variety of birds and wildlife.

Activities

  • Trekking:

    • Offers an exciting 8 km trek through rainforests, considered a perfect spot for adventure enthusiasts. The trek involves both gradual and steep descents to the waterfall, and ascents on the way back. Some sources mention a 1 km trek from Maan village, while others note a 12 km hike.

  • Nature and Wildlife Photography:

    • The surrounding lush greenery and wildlife offer opportunities for photography.

  • Swimming:

    • Although the waterfall features a serene pool, some sources advise against swimming directly under the waterfall due to the powerful and unpredictable force of the water. However, visitor reviews mention enjoying bathing and the chilled water.

  • Best time to visit
    • Monsoon season (June - September) is considered the best time due to full waterfalls and lush greenery.
    • September and October are also mentioned as ideal for viewing the falls, especially on a sunny day.
    • December to January is also mentioned, particularly if interested in seeing the crystal-clear water of the nearby Dawki river.
    • Some advise visiting early morning for the best light and to avoid crowds.

    Safety and guidelines

    • Avoid swimming or bathing directly under the waterfall.
    • Refrain from littering or polluting the area.
    • Do not disturb or feed wildlife.
    • Do not trespass into restricted areas.
    • Avoid using musical instruments or loudspeakers within Wildlife Sanctuaries.
    • Refrain from carrying pets inside Wildlife Sanctuaries.
    • Check weather conditions and warnings before visiting.
    • Do not touch or consume unidentified plants.
    • Supervise children to prevent accidents.
    • Be mindful of others at viewpoints and photo spots.
    • The forest department has deployed personnel and installed safety fencing, but tourists have been observed bypassing them for photos and videos, raising safety concerns.
    • Following a past fatality, the forest department has implemented educational measures and installed warning signs, advising visitors against swimming.

    Nearby attractions

    Several other waterfalls in the vicinity, including Vajrapoha Falls, Chikhale Falls, and Kalasa Falls. Jamboti Hills.
