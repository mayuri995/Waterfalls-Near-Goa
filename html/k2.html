All about Kitwad

Kitwad Falls, also known as Kitwad Waterfalls, is a scenic waterfall located in Kitwad village, Chandgad Taluk, Kolhapur district, Maharashtra. It's a monsoon waterfall, meaning it flows best when the nearby reservoirs are full. The falls are known for their beauty and are a popular spot for visitors, especially during the rainy season.

Key Features:

Location:

Kitwad village, Chandgad Taluk, Kolhapur district, Maharashtra.

Proximity to Belagavi:

About 20 km from Belagavi (Belgaum).

Monsoon Waterfall:

Flows primarily during the rainy season when the reservoirs are full.

Scenic Views:

Offers beautiful views of the waterfall and surrounding greenery.

Accessibility:

Can be reached by parking at the last house in the village and walking along the tank bund and across the water outlet.

Photography:

A good spot for photography, especially from the small bridge over the falls.

Hangout Spot:

A popular place for spending time with friends and family.

Potential for Pollution:

Has experienced issues with pollution due to its popularity, but efforts are being made to maintain cleanliness.

To reach the falls:

  1. Drive to Kitwad village (about 4 km from the main road).
  2. Park your vehicle at the last house in the village.
  3. Walk on the tank bund (reservoir embankment).
  4. Cross the water outlet.
  5. Descend to the waterfall.

Note: Avoid visiting the falls during heavy rains, as the water flow can be strong.
