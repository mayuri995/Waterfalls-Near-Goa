All about Godchinamalaki Waterfall


Godchinamalaki Falls, also known as Markandeya Falls, is a scenic waterfall located in the Belagavi district of Karnataka, India, near Gokak. It's situated on the Markandeya River, cascading down a deep green valley in two distinct steps. The first drop is approximately 25 meters (82 feet) high, followed by a second drop of about 18 meters (59 feet). The falls are known for their picturesque and pleasant scenery, attracting visitors for picnics and enjoyment, especially during the monsoon season. Key features of Godchinamalaki Falls:

Location:

Situated in the Belagavi district, 15 km from Gokak and 70 km from Belagavi city.

River:

The waterfall is formed by the Markandeya River.

Cascading nature:

The water descends in two steps, adding to the visual appeal.

Scenic beauty:

The deep green valley and the cascading water create a picturesque and pleasant atmosphere.

Popular spot:

It's a popular spot for picnics and enjoying nature.

No motorized access:

There is no motorable road directly to the falls, requiring a walk along the river bank.

Monsoon Season:

The falls are particularly attractive during the monsoon season when the water flow is strong.

Nearby Attractions:

Gokak Falls:

Located about 18 kilometers from Godchinamalaki Falls, it's another popular waterfall in the region.

Things to consider:

No basic facilities:

There are limited facilities like shops or toilets near the falls.

Slippery rocks:

The rocks around the falls can be slippery, so caution is advised.

Sudden water flow:

Be aware that the water flow can sometimes increase suddenly.
