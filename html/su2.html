All about Sural Waterfall


Sural Khankira Waterfall, also known as Sural Falls or Surla Falls, is a picturesque waterfall located in the Belgaum district of Karnataka, India, approximately 32 km from Khanapur. It's known for its impressive 300-foot drop and is nestled amidst the lush greenery of the Kankumbi forest region. The waterfall is a popular trekking destination, with trails leading through the Kankumbi forest and along the Kalsa stream. Here's some more information about Sural Khankira Waterfall:

Location:

Sural Falls is located in the Khanapur taluk of Belgaum district, Karnataka, India.

Height:

The waterfall is approximately 300 feet tall.

Best Time to Visit:

The waterfall is most impressive during the monsoon season (June to October) when the water flow is at its peak.

Trekking:

The area around Sural Falls is a popular trekking spot, with trails leading through the Kankumbi forest and along the Kalsa stream.

Cross-State Adventure:

The trek can be a cross-state adventure, as the viewpoint at the final stop is located within the Goa Madie Wildlife Sanctuary, while the waterfall is in Karnataka.

"Venezuela of Karnataka":

The waterfall is also known as the "Venezuela of Karnataka" due to its scenic beauty and impressive views.
